Five data points
Pulled before 8am, before anyone presents a narrative
1
Offer coverage by city by daypart
London 943 venues, 47.5% afternoon, 33% at peak dinner. Manchester 114 venues, 20% Thursday. Bristol 37%, Leeds 45%, Liverpool 40.5%. The time-of-day pattern is the diagnosis.
2
Time-to-close per rep — individual, not average
A 4-day average is either very efficient or cutting corners on qualification. A 21-day average is either complex deals or a stuck pipeline. The individual distribution tells me where coaching goes before a single call-along.
3
Venue activation rate — redemption in last 14 days, by city
A signed venue with no redemption is worse than no venue. It inflates the count and poisons diner trust. This is the number that tells me whether the rep motion is building real supply or adding logos.
4
Visit-to-close ratio per rep
20 visits at 3% conversion is less productive than 12 visits at 18%. High volume, low conversion is a coaching problem disguised as a hustle metric. This surfaces the real quality problem.
5
Weekday liquidity split — Mon-Thu share of redemptions
If 70% of redemptions are Friday-Saturday, venues are giving away margin on covers they would have filled anyway. That is not incremental revenue. This tells me whether the model is actually doing what it promises.
