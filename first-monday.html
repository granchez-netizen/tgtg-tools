Day One · Head of Independent Partners
Five meetings. Five data points.
Five truths before 5pm.
This is the day that sets every expectation the team will have about how you lead. Get the sequence right. Listen more than you speak. Know the numbers before anyone presents them to you.
The Day — Minute by Minute
07:00
Chelsea Kerr — Country Director Before the team
Call Chelsea before anyone else sees you. Her priorities, not yours, shape the first 30 days. Confirm the operating mandate, communication cadence, and one thing she needs from you by end of week one. This is not counted as a meeting — it is the brief before the day starts. The team never knows this happened before them.
Output: Chelsea's definition of a successful day 30. Weekly brief format confirmed. One decision she needs from you this week.
09:00
Team Acquisition Standup 09:00 sharp
You attend — you do not run it. The TL runs it. You observe. How does the TL open the room? Do reps engage or go through the motions? Who speaks first, who goes quiet, what gets celebrated, what gets ignored? The standup tells you more about this team's culture in 15 minutes than any briefing document.
Watch for: energy level, who the natural leader in the room is, whether the TL coaches or just reports numbers.
09:30
Team Account Management Standup 09:30 sharp
Same observation posture. What does the AM TL focus on — partner health metrics or just weekly call counts? Are at-risk accounts named specifically or tracked in aggregate? Is the team proactive or reactive in how they talk about their book?
Watch for: whether churn risk is visible and discussed, or invisible and managed reactively.
10:00
Team Growth Standup 10:00 sharp
The Growth team operates differently — multi-site accounts, non-food segments, longer deal cycles. Does the standup reflect that? Or is it run the same way as the Acquisition standup? A Growth TL who coaches for volume rather than complexity is running the wrong playbook for their team.
Watch for: whether the TL understands what makes a Growth deal different from an Acquisition dial.
10:30
All Reps — Full Team Address 15 minutes only
Not a speech. Four things: who you are, what you believe about sales teams, what they can expect from you, and what you expect from them. Then stop. The team needs to see calm authority — not a vision presentation. End with: "I'm here to listen this week. I'll be sitting with each of you. Tell me what's working and what's getting in your way."
Signal: you are here to make them better, not to reorganise them. Standards will be high. You will be visible on the floor.
11:00
Listen to live calls — phone floor Observe only
Sit on the floor. Listen to three or four live calls across different reps. No feedback today. No coaching today. You are building your own baseline picture of call quality — independent of any AI analysis or TL summary — before anyone has a chance to manage your perception. Your ears first, the data second.
Score privately: does the rep lead with surplus discovery or jump to the pitch? Do they handle objections or back off? Do they close the Surprise Bag window on the call?
13:00
Three Team Leads — Together Group read first
Bring all three TLs into the same room before you meet them individually. Not for detail — for dynamic. Who speaks first? Who defers? Who challenges? The group interaction tells you where the real authority sits in the management layer. Ask one question and let them talk: "What's working well right now, and what's the one thing you'd fix if you could fix it tomorrow?"
Output: first read on TL relationships, confidence levels, and where coaching investment is most needed.
14:30
Pipeline review — with Acquisition TL Data
Pull the live pipeline before this meeting so you arrive with the numbers. Walk through the top 10 open deals together. For each one: what's the next concrete action, when, and who owns it? Any deal without a clear answer gets a 48-hour reactivation deadline or gets closed. This is the first signal to the TL that pipeline hygiene is non-negotiable.
Output: your first read on pipeline quality and the TL's grip on their team's book.
16:00
Bottom quartile rep — informal conversation No manager present
Coffee or a quiet corner. No performance framing. One question: "What would make your week easier?" The rep who is struggling hardest will tell you the most about what the system is failing to provide — if you ask without a manager in the room and without agenda. This is diagnostic, not managerial.
Listen for: skill gap vs motivation gap vs environment gap. The answer shapes what coaching looks like for this person.
17:00
Chelsea — end-of-day brief 5 minutes
Short message or quick call. Not a full debrief — three sentences: what you observed, what you're going to look at more closely, and anything you need from her this week. She hears from you before the day ends. That cadence starts on day one and never breaks.
Signal: Chelsea never has to chase you for an update. Ever.
What You Know Before You Walk In
D
Five data points
Pull these before 8am. Before anyone shapes the narrative.
1
Dials per rep — last 30 days, individual not average
The average hides everything. A 50-dial average could be one rep at 90 and one at 10. You need the distribution to know where effort is actually going.
2
Dial-to-meaningful-conversation ratio per rep
High volume, low meaningful conversations is a pitch quality problem. Low volume, high conversion is a confidence or activity problem. Each needs a different response.
3
Conversation-to-Surprise-Bag-agreed close rate per rep
This is the money metric. A rep who has 20 meaningful conversations and closes 2 has a different problem to one who has 8 and closes 4. Know the number for every rep before you sit with any of them.
4
Partner activation rate — first Surprise Bag live within 48 hours of sign-up
A signed partner who hasn't gone live within 48 hours is a churn risk. This number tells you whether the onboarding handover from Acquisition to AM is working.
5
Partner churn rate — last 90 days by team and by rep
Overall churn is a headline. Churn by rep and by TL tells you whether it's a systemic issue or a specific coaching failure. Pull both.
P
Five people
Who you need a read on by end of day one.
1
All three Team Leads — group first, individual this week
Group read on day one. Individual 1:1s in days 2, 3, and 4. Same question to each: what's working, what isn't, and who on your team would you back to step up?
2
The highest close-rate rep in Acquisition
Not the highest dial count. The highest conversation-to-close rate. This person's method becomes the coaching standard. Listen to their calls before end of week one.
3
The rep who has been here longest
Institutional knowledge and culture carrier. What do they think of the team? What's changed in the last six months? Ask them without a manager present.
4
A rep who joined in the last 8 weeks
Is the onboarding working? What did they find hardest? What would have helped in week one that they didn't have? Their answer is your ramp curriculum audit.
5
The bottom quartile rep
Speak to them without agenda and without a manager. "What would make your week easier?" The answer tells you whether it's a skill gap, a motivation gap, or a system failing them.

T
Five truths
What you know by 5pm that you didn't know at 8am.
1
Whether the best rep's method is documented anywhere
If it lives only in their head, the team is one resignation away from losing its best asset. Finding this out on day one shapes week two's priority.
2
Whether the TLs coach or manage activity
Three standups tells you this. A TL who coaches names specific techniques. A TL who manages activity reads numbers. The difference determines the whole coaching programme.
3
Whether pipeline hygiene is a habit or aspirational
The pipeline review with the Acquisition TL answers this immediately. If they can't tell you the next action on every deal without looking it up, hygiene is aspirational.
4
What the team thinks of their current leadership
Not from the TLs — from the bottom quartile rep conversation and the new starter. Their answers are unfiltered in a way that no structured meeting produces.
5
What Chelsea actually needs from you in the first 30 days
Not what you assumed from the JD. What she said at 7am — in her words. Everything else organises around that.
