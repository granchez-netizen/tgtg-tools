Rep Segmentation — Before Any Training Begins
Quartile the team first.
Then tailor the training.
Generic training delivers generic results. Every rep gets placed into a performance quartile based on dials, meaningful conversations, and Surprise Bag close rate before any training programme starts. The training they receive depends on the quartile they're in — not on how long they've been in the role.
Q1 · Top Performers
The Standard Bearers
Above target on dials, meaningful conversations, and close rate. Consistent week-on-week. Closing Surprise Bags on the call and onboarding on the call.
  • Method extraction: Document their best opening, objection handle, and close. Name it. Add it to the Gold Standard. They teach it at standup.
  • Development path: Where are they heading? TL track, territory expansion, specialist segment lead? Give them the path before they look for it elsewhere.
  • Stretch target: New segment — ethnic food, non-food, multi-site. Their next challenge keeps them engaged.
  • Training role: Call of the Week nominations. Coaching moments in standup. Peer mentoring for Q3 reps.
Retain · Develop · Extract method
Q2 · Solid Performers
The Backbone
Meeting target most weeks. Consistent dialler with good conversation quality. Close rate slightly below Q1 — the gap is usually in the close, not the discovery.
  • Closing focus: The specific skill to develop is closing the Surprise Bag window on the call — not following up tomorrow. Role-play the close in weekly 1:1.
  • AI call analysis: Every call scored. The pattern in the AI feedback tells you what to coach specifically — not what you think the problem is.
  • Paired live listening sessions: Listen to a Q1 rep once per week. Debrief immediately after. What did they do differently at the 4-minute mark?
  • Recognition: Named specifically in standup when they close above their average. The acknowledgement matters — this quartile often goes unrecognised.
Develop · Close coaching · Recognition
Q3 · Below Target
The Coachable
Below target on at least two of three metrics. Not yet a performance concern — a coaching opportunity. The distinction matters: Q3 reps who don't move after 4 weeks of structured coaching become a concern. Before that, they get the full programme.
  • Diagnose first: Skill gap (can't do it), confidence gap (won't try it), or effort gap (isn't doing it)? Each requires a different response.
  • Weekly structured coaching: One specific skill per week. Not "be better at objections" — "when they say 'we don't waste much,' your next line is: tell me about your quietest day."
  • Call-along this week: TL listens to three live calls and gives written feedback within 60 minutes. Not end of day — within the hour.
  • Check-in cadence: TL checks in at 11am and 3pm. Not to pressure — to diagnose. "How's the morning going?" before "what's your dial count?"
Coach · Diagnose · Weekly review
Q4 · Underperforming
Decision Required
Consistently below target across all metrics for 3+ weeks. Coaching has not produced movement. This quartile requires a direct conversation — not more of the same training.
  • Direct conversation this week: "Here's what I'm seeing. Here's what needs to change. Here's the timeline — 3 weeks to reach X." Specific, documented, clear.
  • Diagnose root cause: Wrong role? Personal situation? Management failure? The honest answer changes the response. Don't PIP someone whose problem is a bad onboarding.
  • If skill gap — intensive: Daily 15-minute role-play. Script the specific call. Listen live. Feedback immediately. Repeat.
  • If motivation or fit — honest conversation: Is this the right role for them? Ask it directly. The answer saves everyone time.
Direct conversation · PIP if warranted
How to assign quartiles: Pull the last 4 weeks of data — dials per day, meaningful conversations per 80 dials, Surprise Bags agreed per week. Rank all reps. Top 25% = Q1, next 25% = Q2, next 25% = Q3, bottom 25% = Q4. Review and re-assign every 4 weeks. A rep who moves from Q3 to Q2 is recognised publicly. A rep who moves from Q4 to Q3 gets a specific acknowledgement in their 1:1.
Training Programmes
For the Head of Indies to run with all three Team Leads. These sessions develop the TLs as coaches, not just managers. A TL who can coach call quality, diagnose performance gaps, and develop their team independently is the multiplier that scales the whole function. 8 sessions. Fortnightly. 60–90 minutes each.
For the Head of Indies and TLs to run with all phone reps. 10 sessions across the first 12 weeks. Every session is specific to TGTG's Surprise Bag model — surplus discovery, objection handling, closing on the call, onboarding on the call. Quartile-specific breakouts after every session.
